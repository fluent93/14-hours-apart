# Style Guide

## Tone

- Mature
- Restrained
- Emotionally precise
- Literary but readable
- Romantic but not melodramatic
- Tender but not sentimental
- Cross-cultural without explanation dumps

## Point Of View

Preferred default: close third person.

Use Nathan or Elaine as POV depending on scene pressure. Alternate when useful, but avoid mechanical switching.

## Structure

- Present-day Seoul and New York carry the main story.
- New Jersey flashbacks appear only when they sharpen present conflict.
- Flashbacks should answer emotional questions raised by the present, not interrupt momentum.

## Dialogue

Dialogue should carry subtext. Characters should often avoid saying the exact thing they mean, especially when restraint, pride, guilt, or fear is active.

Avoid:

- Cliche romance speeches
- Explanatory confessions
- Therapy-like clarity too early
- Dialogue that exists only to explain culture or backstory

## Cultural Texture

Show cultural difference through:

- Choices
- Silences
- Habits
- Timing
- Food
- Family expectations
- Institutional behavior
- Misread politeness
- What each character thinks does not need to be said

Avoid:

- Explaining Korean culture to the reader in blocks
- Treating either culture as simple
- Making immigration identity decorative

## Prose Preference

Write with clean sentences, precise images, and controlled emotional escalation. Let one concrete detail carry more weight than several abstract declarations.
