# Characters

## Daniel Ryu

Working name: Daniel Ryu

Background:

- Korean-born man living in Seoul
- Graduated from a Korean university
- Completed mandatory military service
- Went to the United States for study later than typical American college students
- Met Grace at a fictional New Jersey state university
- Returned to Korea and built a serious professional life
- Works in a demanding corporate or leadership environment

Core pressures:

- Responsibility
- Family obligation
- Career fatigue
- Emotional restraint
- Midlife uncertainty
- The cost of changing a life that appears stable from the outside

Emotional arc:

Daniel moves from endurance and responsibility toward vulnerability and choice. He must learn that choosing love is not the same as escaping duty, and that silence can wound even when it is meant as restraint.

Watch-outs:

- Do not make Daniel a heroic sufferer.
- Do not let his longing erase Grace's agency.
- Avoid self-explanatory monologues.

## Grace Cho

Working name: Grace Cho

Background:

- Korean-born woman living in New York
- Immigrated to the United States around ninth grade
- Completed high school, college, graduate school, and career formation in the U.S.
- Senior academic leader at a fictional New York or East Coast college
- Built an independent, accomplished life in America

Core pressures:

- Self-made independence
- Control
- Ambition
- Institutional responsibility
- Immigrant reinvention
- Fear of becoming emotionally dependent on a life she cannot control

Emotional arc:

Grace moves from self-protection and control toward allowing love to disrupt the life she built. She must decide whether independence means never needing anyone, or being free enough to choose need.

Watch-outs:

- Do not make Grace only an object of longing.
- Do not over-explain her American life.
- Do not use private details unless fictionalized and cleared.

## Relationship Dynamic

Daniel and Grace loved each other before they had the courage, language, or life stability to understand what love required. Their present-day intimacy grows through calls, messages, time-zone rituals, humor, shared memory, and the disciplined restraint of two people old enough to know what damage desire can do.

They are not asking whether love exists. They are asking what it can responsibly become.
