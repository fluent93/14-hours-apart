# Privacy Rules

## Core Rule

This project is inspired by real emotional material, but it must be written as fiction.

## Do Not Use

- Real names
- Exact job titles if they identify real people
- Exact institutions where identification creates privacy risk
- Real private conversations in transcript-like form
- Exact dates, sequences, or locations that expose private people
- Personal details from Elaine's life unless explicitly cleared and fictionalized

## Required Transformations

Transform, compress, fictionalize, or omit:

- Private conversations
- Identifying professional details
- Family details
- Medical, legal, financial, or personal crisis details
- Any third-party identifying information

## Institution Rule

Rutgers should become a fictional New Jersey state university unless the author later decides otherwise.

## Character Rule

The central characters should be treated as fictionalized characters. Current working names are Nathan Kim and Elaine Park. Victor Han is a composite character for the older Korean graduate student.

## Reader-Facing Rule

The story should not read like a confession, memoir, or self-explanation. It should serve English-language romance readers first.
