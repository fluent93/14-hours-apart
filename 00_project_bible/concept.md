# Concept

## Logline

Twenty years after a brief, unfinished love at a New Jersey university, Nathan Kim in Seoul and Elaine Park in New York reconnect across a fourteen-hour time difference and discover that loving each other may be easier than changing the lives they built apart.

## Core Premise

Nathan and Elaine are Korean-born, successful, and no longer young enough to pretend love exists outside consequence. Their reconnection is tender and electric, but the real conflict is practical, moral, and emotional: career, family, immigration history, aging, obligation, and the quiet pride of people who survived by becoming self-contained.

## Central Dramatic Question

Can two people who lost each other twenty years ago change the lives they built without each other?

## Thematic Line

They lost each other once because they were too young to choose love. Twenty years later, they may lose each other again because their lives are too complete.

## Genre Promise

This is a mature second-chance romance with literary restraint and commercial readability. The reader should feel longing, intimacy, regret, humor, pressure, and the ache of adult choice.

## Not This

- Not a memoir
- Not a confession
- Not a translation
- Not a revenge story
- Not a simple fantasy of escape
- Not a story where one person exists only as the other's lost dream
