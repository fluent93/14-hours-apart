# Timeline

## Past

- Nathan is born and raised in Korea.
- Elaine is born in Korea and immigrates to the United States around ninth grade.
- Nathan graduates from a Korean university and completes mandatory military service.
- Nathan goes to the United States for later study.
- Nathan and Elaine meet at a fictional New Jersey state university.
- Their relationship is brief, intense, and unresolved.
- Misunderstanding, immaturity, timing, or unspoken fear separates them.
- Nathan returns to Korea and builds his life in Seoul.
- Elaine remains in the United States and builds her academic career.

## Gap Years

Approximately twenty years pass.

Nathan and Elaine become people whose lives are full: work, obligations, habits, professional reputations, social expectations, and private compromises.

## Present Frame

- Nathan lives in Seoul.
- Elaine lives in New York.
- They reconnect through an alumni-related trigger, professional connection, old message, online event, photograph, or chance digital encounter.
- Present-day calls happen across Korea and the U.S.
- Seoul and New York are fourteen hours apart in winter and thirteen hours apart in summer.

## Symbolic Time Rule

The changing time difference can mark emotional weather:

- Winter: fourteen hours, distance, restraint, the cost of delay
- Summer: thirteen hours, false closeness, the temptation to believe one hour changes everything

Possible line:

> In summer, it was thirteen hours. In winter, fourteen. But neither of them believed an hour could make that much difference until it did.
