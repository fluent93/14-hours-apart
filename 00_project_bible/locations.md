# Locations

## Seoul

Present-day Nathan setting.

Represents:

- Night
- Work
- Duty
- Family
- Accumulated life
- Emotional restraint
- A life one cannot easily abandon

Use through:

- Late office lights
- Commutes after dark
- Quiet apartments
- Family meals
- Corporate rituals
- Winter streets
- Messages sent when New York is waking

## New York

Present-day Elaine setting.

Represents:

- Morning
- Reinvention
- Immigration
- Independence
- Ambition
- The life one built by leaving

Use through:

- Early campus meetings
- Coffee before sunrise
- Apartment windows
- Subway movement
- Academic offices
- Public competence and private fatigue
- Messages received when Seoul should be asleep

## Fictional New Jersey State University

Past setting.

Represents:

- Youth
- First freedom
- First misunderstanding
- The unfinished self
- A borrowed American landscape where both characters were becoming someone else

Rules:

- Do not use Rutgers by default.
- Use a fictional institution unless the author later decides otherwise.
- Avoid identifying dorms, departments, professors, or exact campus geography.

Possible placeholder names:

- Garden State University
- North Jersey State
- Raritan State College
- Hudson-Raritan University

## Location Priority Rule

The reader should experience the novel primarily as a Seoul/New York story. New Jersey is not the main stage; it is the memory field where the relationship began. Flashbacks should be selective and should intensify present-day Seoul/New York choices.
